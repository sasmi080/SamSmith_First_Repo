Shadow Mobile App

A mobile solution to build new and long lasting mentorships.

My Role: Project Manager, UX Researcher & Designer, Presenter

Duration: Three week design sprint

Tools Used: Figma, Miro, Google Drive, and Zoom

Problem

Traditional, and now virtual networking, often leaves individuals feeling exhausted and with little to show for their efforts.

Solution

Develop a mentoring app that will help individuals looking to change industries or grow within their current industry through networking.

Impact

Users feel empowered to initiate conversations with potential mentors and therefore grow within or outside of their industries.

RESEARCH

How might we improve a users experience to successfully match and make a long term mentorship?

Survey

We conducted a survey of users to understand how they use exsiting networking apps or events. From this we learned that 73% saw career growth opportunities present themselves because of conversational networking.

Our key takway was - networking works! But it can be tricky.

User Interviews

Next, we completed 5 user interviews to gain deeper insights.

Those interviewed expressed that they believed in networking, but find blindly reaching out to someone uncomfortable, and that it does not typically result in a response or a mentorship being built.

"I wish I had a mentor to help me with my career trajectory."

"I wish people would review my profile in depth, and respond to messages."

"What if there were an app where mentors could reach out first?"

From these interviews we learned that people want to build lasting mentorships, but do not have a way to do so. The main frustrations are the lack of response to messages, the inability to schedule time directly with potential mentors, and the awkwardness of having to blindly reach out.

Our team developed this persona from our user research based on trends, motivations, and pain points we uncovered. Claire guided our design process and decisions moving forward.

ITERATION & DESIGN

Definition

With our user persona Claire in mind we conducted an “I like, I wish, I wonder” excersie and created a feature prioroitization matrix to determine the minimum viable product. After defining our main features we developed a user flow to map out the key steps a user would take, and we determined the number of screens that need to be designed.

With a three week timeframe for this project we focused on developing the onboarding flow for a new user, as well as the scheduling a video chat with a mentor flow as our main features.

Paper Sketches

Our team starated with paper sketches based on the user flow we developed. This offered us some guidance on how the pages would be laid out, and gave our team the ability to collaborate on the initial design.

Low-Fidelty Wireframes

Using Figma our team created wireframes that focused on layout and copy placement. We used these wireframes to test and iterate on user flow. We learned that many users preferred a calendar view for scheduling appointments, would like the option of a video chat along with messaging with the mentor, and wanted to be able to rate the mentor after a call.

High-Fidelty Wireframes

After conducting multiple user tests and iterating on our main features and user flow, we began adding in our style choices. We selected the color green to represent growth, added in a pop-up modal post call to review the experience, and applied iOS features to the wireframes.

Prototype

Our final design addressed the users desire to network and start mentorships, but helped in overcoming the fear of blindly reaching out.

With our user flow including direct messaging and scheduling for a video call, users felt more empowered to reach out and start building mentorships.
